WCAG Guidelines

What is Contrast Ratio?

Contrast ratio is a measure of the luminance difference between foreground and background colors. It ranges from 1:1 (no contrast) to 21:1 (maximum contrast between pure black and white). Higher ratios mean better readability.

WCAG AA vs AAA

Level AA (Minimum): 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text. This is the legally required standard in many countries.

Level AAA (Enhanced): 7:1 for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text. Provides the best accessibility but may limit design choices.

Large vs Normal Text

Large text: 18pt (24px) or larger, or 14pt (18.5px) bold or larger. Requires less contrast because it's easier to read.

Normal text: Any text smaller than the large text threshold. Requires higher contrast ratios.

Beyond Color Contrast

Remember that color alone should not convey information. Use icons, patterns, or text labels alongside color. Ensure interactive elements have visible focus states and sufficient target sizes.
